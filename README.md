# Customer Shopping Behavior Analysis

## Overview

This project is an end-to-end Data Analytics project focused on understanding customer shopping behavior using retail transaction data. The analysis aims to uncover purchasing patterns, customer demographics, product performance, subscription trends, and revenue drivers that can support data-driven business decisions.

The project demonstrates the complete analytics workflow, including data cleaning, exploratory data analysis (EDA), database integration, SQL analysis, dashboard development, business reporting, and presentation creation.

---

## Dataset

The dataset contains approximately 3,900 customer transaction records with information such as:

* Customer ID
* Age
* Gender
* Item Purchased
* Category
* Purchase Amount (USD)
* Location
* Review Rating
* Subscription Status
* Discount Applied
* Payment Method
* Previous Purchases
* Frequency of Purchases

The data was used to analyze customer behavior, spending patterns, product performance, and subscription engagement.

## Results & Insights

### Key Findings

* Clothing generated the highest revenue and sales volume.
* Customers aged 46+ contributed the highest revenue.
* Loyal and repeat customers represented a significant share of total revenue.
* Subscription customers demonstrated stronger engagement and repeat purchase behavior.
* Customer satisfaction averaged 3.75/5 based on review ratings.

### Business Recommendations

* Expand customer loyalty programs.
* Increase subscription adoption through incentives.
* Focus marketing efforts on high-performing customer segments.
* Prioritize inventory for top-performing product categories.
* Use customer segmentation for targeted marketing campaigns.
